# 🔮 Grimarcana

> **Decode at your own risk.**

Grimarcana is a Minecraft mod focused on **discovery-driven magic**, where players do not unlock spells through recipes or tech trees, but instead **decode a lost arcane language** through research, experimentation, and interpretation.

Knowledge is imperfect.  
Magic is unstable.  
And the world reacts to your mistakes.

---

## 🧠 Core Concept

In Grimarcana, you are not learning magic—you are **trying to understand it**.

- There are no static recipes
- There is no guaranteed correctness
- You can be **wrong**

Players must gather fragments of knowledge, interpret symbolic systems, and apply what they *believe* is correct—sometimes with dangerous consequences.

---

## 🔁 Gameplay Loop

1. 🌍 **Explore**
   - Discover research fragments, artifacts, and environmental clues

2. 🧩 **Research**
   - Solve translation puzzles using fragments and symbols

3. 🧠 **Interpret**
   - Form hypotheses about how magic works

4. 🔮 **Apply**
   - Use your understanding in alchemy, runes, and other systems

5. ⚠️ **Risk**
   - Misinterpretation leads to instability

6. 💥 **Consequence**
   - Failures range from minor to catastrophic

7. 🔁 **Refine**
   - Learn, adapt, and correct your understanding

---

## 📖 Research System

### 🧩 Fragment-Based Discovery
Players collect **Research Fragments** from:
- Exploration (biomes, ruins, structures)
- Experimentation (successes and failures)
- Observation (environment and entities)

Fragments are intentionally vague and must be interpreted.

---

### 🔣 Symbolic Language
Magic is built on a system of **core symbols** (e.g., Heat, Stability, Chaos, Life, Decay).

- Symbols represent concepts, not items
- They interact with each other through hidden rules
- Their meanings must be discovered and verified

---

### 🧠 Translation Puzzle
Research is performed through a **grid-based interface**:

- Early game: 2×2 grid  
- Mid game: 3×3 grid  
- Late game: 4×4+ grid  

Players construct “magical sentences” using fragments and symbols.

---

### 🎲 Outcomes

Research is not binary:

- ✅ **Correct** — unlocks true knowledge  
- 🟡 **Partial** — incomplete understanding  
- ❌ **Incorrect** — false knowledge (critical system)  
- ⚠️ **Failure** — no progress  
- 💥 **Catastrophic Failure** — triggers instability  

---

### ⚠️ Incorrect Knowledge

Players can learn the **wrong interpretation** of a system.

This leads to:
- Spells that behave unexpectedly
- Subtle inefficiencies
- Dangerous outcomes later in progression

Players must detect and correct these through experimentation and observation.

---

## 🔄 Rune System

Players generate **orbiting runes** that represent active magical effects.

### Features:
- Real-time visual feedback
- Stability reflected through motion:
  - Stable → smooth orbit  
  - Unstable → flickering  
  - Critical → erratic / colliding  

### Gameplay Impact:
- Runes store charges and effects
- Can interact with other systems
- Influence research outcomes when nearby

---

## ⚠️ Instability System

Every magical action has:
- **Power**
- **Instability**

### Outcome Spectrum:
- Perfect  
- Imperfect  
- Unstable  
- Failure  
- Catastrophic  

### Progression Scaling:
- Early game → safe experimentation  
- Mid game → hazards and enemies  
- Late game → major consequences  
- End game → reality distortion  

---

## ☣️ World Reactivity

Magic affects the environment:

- Corruption systems (spreading instability)
- Terrain mutation
- Magical anomaly zones
- Mutated entities

---

## ⚖️ XP System (Stabilization)

XP is used to **manage risk**, not just progression.

### Uses:
- 🛡️ Pre-cast stabilization  
- ⚡ Reactive stabilization  
- ☢️ Catastrophe mitigation  

### Advanced:
- Non-linear scaling
- XP burn vs pool
- Overdraw (temporary debt)

---

## 🎮 Progression

### Early Game
- Learn basic symbols
- Low-risk experimentation

### Mid Game
- Unlock disciplines
- Encounter meaningful consequences

### Late Game
- Complex systems
- High instability
- World becomes reactive

## 🎨 Design Philosophy

Grimarcana is built on:

- **Discovery over instruction**
- **Interpretation over certainty**
- **Systems over content**
- **Failure as a learning tool**
